# CYBER CRIME CASE STUDY: PHISHING
## What is phishing?
Phishing is a cyber fraud where attackers impersonate trusted entities (banks, apps, or colleges) to trick users into revealing sensitive data like passwords, OTPs, or card details.

These scams usually happen through:

- Fake phone calls
- SMS
- Fake websites
- Pop-up adds

## How the fraud happens? (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker contacts the victim claiming an issue or urgent transaction or sharing otp.
2. The victim is asked to click a malicious link or approve a payment request.
3. The attacker may ask for OTP or PIN (which should never be shared).
4. Once approved/shared, money is instantly transferred to the attacker.

## Who are targeted?
- Students
- Elderly people
- Users unaware of online scams

## Consequences:
- Financial loss
- Identity theft
- Account hacking
- Privacy breach

## Key takeaways:
_**Verify links, never share OTP/passwords, check sender authenticity, and enable 2-factor authentication**_

**AWARENESS AND CAUTION ARE THE BEST DEFENSES AGAINST PHISHING!!**
